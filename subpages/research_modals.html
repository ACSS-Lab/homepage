Autonomous Decision-Making under Environment & System Uncertainties

Safety vs. Multimodality in Generative AI Planning

Broad Problem: In robot planning problems, generative AI methods (diffusion models, flow-matching, etc.) can represent multimodal behaviors (e.g., a robot arm picking up a cup in multiple different positions). However, this flexibility conflicts inherently with safety, stability, and related guarantees crucial to closed-loop control.
Research Goal: Develop generative AI-based planning algorithms for autonomous (robotic) agents that fundamentally encode safety/stability constraints without losing multimodal flexibility. Current methods of investigation include designing hierarchical safety filters and distributional control formulations.
Application Areas: Generative AI for robotics; Diffusion/Flow-matching; Safe/Robust autonomy; Trajectory and motion planning.

Robust & Adaptive Control with Uncertainty Quantification

Broad Problem: Real-world autonomous systems often experience external environment uncertainties and unmodeled dynamics. In particular, when stochastic effects are nonstationary (e.g., highly-dynamic environments, interaction-awareness), many stochastic control algorithms can experience poor adaptability and robustness.
Research Goal: Develop path-planners that are robust and adaptive especially against nonstationary uncertainties, by combining both model-based and data-driven methods. Current methods of investigation include classical set prediction & propagation methods, learning-based OOD detectors (e.g., random network distillation), and online reinforcement learning.
Application Areas: Fault-tolerant systems; OOD detection for control; Autonomous trajectory and motion planning; Online reinforcement learning.

Large-Scale Control and Estimation for Networks / Multiagent Systems

Robust & Adaptive Large-Scale Network Control

Broad Problem: Scalable implementations of stochastic control and state-estimation algorithms introduce additional uncertainties (e.g., dynamic graph topology, delayed information transmission among agents) and constraints (e.g., limited communication bandwidth, lack of coordination among agents).
Research Goal: Develop distributed/decentralized stochastic control and state-estimation algorithms for large-scale systems that are robust, adaptive, and scalable (i.e., localizable) to high-dimensional network uncertainties. Current methods of investigation include system level synthesis, consensus methods, and others.
Application Areas: Multi-agent systems; Large-scale networks; Fault-tolerant control; Consensus; System level synthesis.

Situational Awareness with Wireless Sensor Networks

Broad Problem: Real-time situational awareness for large-scale environments requires a delicate balance between efficiency and accuracy. However, many conventional data-gathering architectures have the external sensors transmit all their measurements to the decision-making central agent, increasing traffic congestion and unnecessarily redundant communications. In the case of visual data, high computation and power demands for processing can cause large reconstruction errors from asynchronous sensor updates.
Research Goal: Develop architectures for large-scale, distributed estimation and scene reconstruction, with special focus on improving data preprocessing and transmission to optimally balance network efficiency and good reconstruction performance. Current methods of investigation include sensor scheduling, and 3D Gaussian splats.
Application Areas: Distributed data-gathering; Multi-target Tracking; Distributed 3D reconstruction; Sensor networks; Robotic sensor networks.

Data-Efficient Online Learning for Adaptive Autonomous Systems

Control-Relevant Latent Representations for Embodied AI

Broad Problem: AGI architectures (e.g., VLAMs) offer a powerful foundation for complex autonomous decision-making and embodied AI. However, their internal representations are often difficult to analyze, especially when 1) conditions like visual inputs, language inputs, or contexts change, and when 2) when tasks must be performed sequentially over time without catastrophic forgetting.
Research Goal: Develop control-relevant latent representations and geometric models of the latent space for VLAM-based decision makers. Develop, in particular, a "similarity metric" on the latent space so that semantically-equivalent observations remain close. Current methods of investigation include manifold steering, dimensionality reduction, and group equivariance.
Application Areas: Vision-language-action models; World models; Embodied AI; Huamn-robot interaction; Continuous Control; Representation learning.

Data-Efficient Adaptation and Active Learning

Broad Problem: Real-world data collection for robotic control is often expensive, sparse, and safety-critical. This means naive methods of fine-tuning and training are often unsuitable for robotic systems, especially if they must learn to adapt to new tasks and environments.
Research Goal: Develop methods for improving robot performance by identifying the most valuable data samples and allowing agents to make decisions quickly (with accelerated convergence towards the optimal hyperparameters). Current methods of investigation include adaptive gradient descent, active learning, and stochastic optimization.
Application Areas: Optimization methods in ML; Active learning; Online learning; Stochastic Approximation; Data-efficient control.
